Research

Currently a Professor in the Department of Physics at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee
  • Transient Collaboration for the Murchison Widefield Array
  • Australia Square Kilometer Array Pathfinder Variables and Slow Transients Survey
  • Optical/infrared/X-ray observations of young neutron stars
  • Observations of compact white dwarf binary systems
  • Until 2010 , I was a Hubble Postdoctoral Fellow at the Kavli Institute for Theoretical Physics and at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology .
  • Until 2007 , I was a Pappalardo Fellow in astrophysics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology .
  • I work with Lars Bildsten 's group.
  • I work with Deepto Chakrabarty 's group.
  • I also work with Marten van Kerkwijk of the University of Toronto
  • Until 2004, I was a graduate student in astrophysics at the California Institute of Technology :
  • I worked with Shri Kulkarni 's group.
  • Thesis projects:
    • A search for compact central objects in shell supernova remnants (SNRs).
    • An investigation of the multi-wavelength properties of nearby, isolated, thermally emitting neutron stars (INSs, also called RQNS, DTNS, etc.).
    • A GBT Spectrometer Spigot Card for Pulsar Observations . This is the facility search instrument at the GBT (and has some timing capabilities too).
  • I am mostly observationally / instrumentally based. When given the chance, I have observed with
    • The Palomar 200 inch and 60 inch.
    • Keck
    • Magellan
    • Arecibo
    • The VLA
    • plus data from HST , CXO , XMM , Spitzer and others
